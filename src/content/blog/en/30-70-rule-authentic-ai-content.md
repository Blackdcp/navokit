This week, a developer named Simon Willison built an "LLM cliché highlighter." Its only job is to scan text and highlight words like *delve, tapestry, testament to*, and *realm*. 

The tool went viral for a simple reason: **AI content fatigue is real.**

When generating content costs zero, generic content is worth zero. If you let ChatGPT write your social media posts from scratch, you will be instantly recognized as a bot and scrolled past. 

But abandoning AI entirely means falling behind. The solution is what we call the **30/70 Rule of AI Content**.

## The 30/70 Rule

To win in the feed today, your content must be **30% AI formatting and 70% human insight**.

- **The 70% (You):** The spicy opinion, the personal failure, the raw data, the messy shower thought. AI cannot invent these.
- **The 30% (AI):** The formatting, the platform-specific structure (e.g., turning a block of text into an X thread vs. a LinkedIn post), spelling, and hooks.

When you flip this ratio (70% AI, 30% You), you get a cliché generator. 

## The "De-AI" Checklist for Social Posts

Before you hit publish on any AI-assisted post, run it through this 5-point checklist:

- [ ] **The "In Conclusion" Test:** Did the AI add a summary paragraph at the end? Delete it. Social posts don't need conclusions.
- [ ] **The Vocabulary Test:** Remove words like *crucial, multifaceted, landscape, delve, tapestry*.
- [ ] **The Empathy Test:** Does it sound like you are talking to a colleague on Slack, or presenting a whitepaper to the UN?
- [ ] **The formatting check:** Are there too many random emojis? Limit them to 1-2 per post.
- [ ] **The Hook Check:** Is the first sentence something only *you* could say?
