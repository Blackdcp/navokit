## Why AI video prompts need structure

Text-to-video tools work best when the prompt describes one clear shot. A useful prompt usually includes the subject, action, setting, camera movement, lighting, and visual style.

If the prompt is too broad, the output may feel random. If the prompt describes too many scenes at once, the model may compress or ignore parts of the idea.

## Prompt formula

Use this structure:

```text
Subject + action + setting + camera movement + lighting + style
```

For example:

```text
A red fox walking through a snowy forest, soft snowfall, low tracking shot, realistic cinematic style.
```

## Product shot examples

```text
A perfume bottle on black stone, mist drifting around it, slow product shot, dramatic lighting.
```

```text
A ceramic coffee cup on a wooden desk, morning light through a window, slow push-in camera, calm lifestyle style.
```

Product prompts work best when the object, surface, lighting, and movement are all specific.

## Social clip examples

```text
A cozy desk setup at night, laptop screen glowing, coffee steam rising, slow push-in camera, warm light.
```

```text
A creator packing a small camera bag on a clean table, overhead shot, fast but smooth motion, natural daylight.
```

For social clips, keep the idea simple. One strong visual is usually better than a long scene description.

## Cinematic scene examples

```text
A lone cyclist crossing a foggy bridge at sunrise, wide shot, soft blue light, cinematic realism.
```

```text
Rain falling on a neon street at night, reflections on wet asphalt, slow dolly movement, moody film look.
```

Cinematic prompts benefit from mood, camera movement, and lighting details.
