## Start with one specific idea

Bad input:

```text
Write a social media post about productivity.
```

That prompt is too broad, so the result will probably sound like every other productivity post.

Better input:

```text
I built a free tool that turns Markdown into clean image cards. The audience is creators, developers, and indie founders who are tired of sharing messy screenshots. Keep the tone practical and direct. Mention paste Markdown, preview, export PNG.
```

This gives the draft a real job: who it is for, what changed, and what the reader can do.

## Review facts before tone

Before polishing the style, check whether the draft is true.

Look for:

- invented numbers
- exaggerated results
- fake user claims
- unsupported "best" or "fastest" statements
- features your product does not actually have
- platform names you did not ask for
- dates, pricing, or availability details that may be wrong

If a draft says "increase engagement by 300%" and you do not have evidence, delete it. If it says "works for every business," narrow the claim. Social posts do not need fake certainty to be useful.

## Edit AI drafts into human posts

Most AI drafts need less polish, not more.

| AI draft problem | Human edit |
| --- | --- |
| "In today's fast-paced digital world..." | Cut it. Start with the real point. |
| Broad promise | Replace it with one use case. |
| Too many adjectives | Keep the nouns and verbs. |
| Generic hook | Use a specific frustration, mistake, or observation. |
| Too promotional | Add what changed, what you learned, or what the user can do. |

The final post should feel edited, not generated.

## Platform-specific editing notes

For X / Twitter, lead with the idea. Long setup usually weakens the post. If you include a link, explain the value before the link.

For LinkedIn, add context. Explain what changed, why it matters, and what others can learn. Keep the tone useful instead of self-congratulatory.

For Instagram, match the caption to the visual. If the asset is a generated video, image card, or product screenshot, the caption should support the asset rather than repeat every detail.

For hook ideas, generate more than you need. Choose the hook that is accurate, not merely dramatic.

## A repeatable NavoKit workflow

Use this sequence:

1. Write one clear idea.
2. Paste the idea into the [AI Social Post Generator](/en/tools/ai-social-booster).
3. Generate the draft types you need.
4. Pick the closest version.
5. Check facts, claims, and platform fit.
6. Rewrite the first line.
7. Add one real detail.
8. Remove generic AI-sounding phrases.
9. Pair the post with a real asset when possible.
10. Publish only when the post still feels true.

If the idea is visual, create the asset first. You can turn notes into an image card with [Markdown to Image](/en/tools/markdown-to-image), or generate a short clip with the [Free AI Video Generator](/en/tools/free-ai-video-generator). Then use NavoKit to draft the post around the asset.

## Privacy and sensitive information

Your input is processed by an AI service to generate the draft. Do not paste private customer details, unreleased financial data, API keys, passwords, confidential roadmaps, or internal messages.

If a post is based on client work, anonymize the example before using AI. If a post mentions performance, revenue, legal, medical, or financial outcomes, verify the claim manually before publishing.

## Final checklist

Before publishing, ask:

- Is the main idea clear in the first few lines?
- Is every factual claim true?
- Does the tone match my normal voice?
- Is the platform format appropriate?
- Did I remove generic AI-sounding phrases?
- Is there one real detail or example?
- Does the attached image, video, or link support the post?
- Would I still stand behind this tomorrow?

AI can speed up the blank-page stage. The final post should still carry your judgment.
